Making this website

The process

I was bored after two days of winter break. I'd had an idea to make my own personal website after learning some HTML during the Boilermake Hackathon. I finally had the time so I committed myself to learning how to do it.

The first thing I did was some google searches, which I will call "research" to sound more professional. I figured out that I could, after buying the domain I wanted, host the site on my Github, which would make alterations to the site quick and painless, as well as free.

Because I do not have any confidence in my powers of design, I bootlegged the format of the site from colorlib. From there, I had to work through replacing the sections to apply to the information I wanted it to represent, which involved flexing my html and css knowledge.

Finally, I am currently improving the contact me section such that the user can send messages through some php scripts.
